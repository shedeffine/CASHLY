How to Improve Your Scam Detection Skills

1

Verify sender information: Legitimate organizations use official numbers/addresses. Scammers often use similar-looking addresses with slight misspellings.

2

Look for urgency and pressure: Scammers often create a false sense of urgency to make you act without thinking.

3

Check for poor grammar and spelling: While some scams are well-written, many contain grammatical errors and awkward phrasing.

4

Be wary of requests for personal information: Legitimate organizations won't ask for passwords, PINs, or verification codes via message.

5

Don't click on suspicious links: Hover over links to see the actual URL before clicking. When in doubt, go directly to the official website.
